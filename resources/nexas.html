The war between rivals

While there was both Connex and M>Train around, due to being rival companies, always wanted to outdo each other, the Comeng refurbishment was a failure due to the refurbishments being overall very similar, so when it came time to replace the aging fleet of the Hitachi's, M>Train chose to buy a Modular Metro from Siemens.
The Modular Metro M>Train chose was the Nexas, boasting wider plug doors (doors that close like a plug), a wider hallway, 2x2 seating and articulated gangways, this train was made very much ahead of its time and was due to outdo Connex's X'trapolis.

The fallback

When the first Nexas arrived M>Train found it was a little bit too long for their shortened platforms, so had to invest in getting the platforms to be a little bit longer to fit their new large trains.
While M>Train were able to finish this project, they never got to see their Nexas enter passenger service due to National Express (The company who owned M>Train) calling for bankrupcy and pulling out.

The Connex days

Connex got handed the Melbourne network franchise, and all of M>Train's assets, including the Nexas, Connex managed to get the nexas out onto the network in passenger services, and had a bid of which train would become the backbone of the fleet, the Nexas unfortunately were unable to be built fast enough so only a handful of new ones were built for the south-east lines and then ended the production of Nexas's.

after a few months of the Nexas running in passenger services, there was a lot of recorded instances of the nexas's over-shooting platforms, and even some Nexas's that were in stabling yards, running by themselves and crashing into infrastructure.
