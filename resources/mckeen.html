The Railmotor

The First self-propelled Railmotor to be in Australia, made in America; Victorian Railways chose to trial these due to being far easier to maintain as well as easier to drive, without having to manually shovel coal into any firebox.

The Mckeen Railmotor ran for a short period of time, due to being unreliable, being de-engined in 1919 and used as carriages instead on the Altona line until 1926.

The American Variants

While the Mckeen Railmotor's were a failure in Victoria, Australia, they were less of a failure in their homeland of America, with them surviving up into the 1930's and living on long enough to be fully restored and working into the modern day
